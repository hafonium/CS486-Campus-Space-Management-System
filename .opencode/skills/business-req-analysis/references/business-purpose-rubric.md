# Business Purpose Rubric

## Goal
Write a concise 2–3 sentence paragraph that explains:
- why the organization needs the system,
- what operational problem(s) it solves,
- what business outcome(s) it supports.

## Include
A strong business-purpose paragraph should include:
- the organizational need,
- the current pain point or inefficiency,
- the intended improvement or outcome.

## Avoid
Do not:
- restate the requirement as a feature list,
- mention entities, tables, SQL, or implementation details,
- repeat long background text,
- paraphrase the brief sentence by sentence.

## Good pattern
"The system is needed to centralize and control the organization’s current manual process. It solves operational issues such as inconsistent handling, lack of visibility, or avoidable errors. It supports better coordination, accountability, and historical tracking."

## Bad pattern
"This system manages users, requests, approvals, history, and reporting."

Why this is weak:
- it lists features,
- it does not explain the organizational problem,
- it does not state the intended outcome.

## Self-check
Before finalizing, ask:
1. Did I explain why the system is needed?
2. Did I explain what problem it solves?
3. Did I explain what outcome it supports?
4. Does this sound like a business summary rather than a feature list?
